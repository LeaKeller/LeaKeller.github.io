Control-theoretic methods and agebraic geometric approaches to biochemical reaction networks

Understand bio-chemical reaction networks through the comparative study of different oscillatory dynamical systems.

This project deals with the Goodwin model of circadian rythm and three reduced models of the Belousov-Zhabotinski chemical reactions. They are examined and compared by applying dynamical systems theory and control theoretical tools. More precisely, stability analysis of equilibria and Hopf bifurcations. Moreover, we investigate the issues such as positivity and boundeness of the trajectories. The work is carried out by mathematical analysis and by numerical simulations performed in Matlab. In particular, we provide a complete parameter description on stability of the Goodwin model in terms of the Hill number, which seems non-addressed in the literature.
